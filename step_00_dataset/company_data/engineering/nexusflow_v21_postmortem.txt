POST-MORTEM: NexusFlow v2.1 Ingestion Outage
Date of Incident: August 14, 2023
Date of Report: August 21, 2023
Author: Yuki Tanaka, Lead, NexusFlow Team
Status: Final
Severity: SEV-1
Duration: 4h 23min (09:15 PST – 13:38 PST)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------------
On August 14, 2023, a misconfigured rate limiter introduced in the NexusFlow v2.1 release caused a major outage in the data ingestion service, owned by the Data Platform Team. The misconfiguration set max_connections=10 instead of the intended max_connections=1000, which throttled inbound producer connections almost immediately after deployment. Downstream impact extended to InsightLens, which depends on the NexusFlow events_api for analytics ingestion. PulseConnect was unaffected.

Customer impact: ingestion throughput dropped to ~1% of nominal. Buffered producers retried successfully after restoration; no data loss was observed beyond extended ingestion latency. We are following up directly with Phoenix Corp regarding SLA impact under their 99.99% uptime commitment.

13:38 — Full restoration confirmed across all regions. Incident closed.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
ROOT CAUSE
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The v2.1 release included a refactor of the ingestion service's connection pooling layer. As part of that change, the rate limiter configuration file was updated. The intended value `max_connections: 1000` was committed as `max_connections: 10`. The change passed code review, unit tests, and staging deployment — staging traffic volume was insufficient to exceed 10 concurrent producer connections, so the misconfiguration did not manifest until production load was applied.

Contributing factors:
1. No production-traffic canary stage before full rollout.
2. Config values were not bounded-checked against expected operational ranges.
3. Staging load profile does not approximate production producer concurrency.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT WENT WELL
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Alerts fired within 8 minutes of deployment.
- Cross-team coordination between Data Platform, NexusFlow, and InsightLens was effective.
- Rollback tooling, while imperfect, eventually resolved the situation without data loss.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT WENT POORLY
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Rollback was slower than acceptable due to stuck pods.
- Config validation gap allowed an obviously incorrect value to ship.
- Staging did not reflect production traffic shape.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
ACTION ITEMS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
AI-1 (Owner: Priya Nair, Due: Sept 8) — Add a pre-deploy config validation step that enforces declared min/max bounds on all rate limiter and connection pool parameters. Block deploys on violation.

AI-2 (Owner: Yuki Tanaka, Due: Sept 22) — Implement automated canary testing for NexusFlow ingestion: route 5% of production traffic to new builds for 30 minutes with auto-rollback on SLO regression.

AI-3 (Owner: Marcus Webb, Due: Sept 15) — Update the engineering deployment checklist to require explicit reviewer sign-off on changed config keys, separate from code review.

AI-4 (Owner: Aisha Johnson, Due: Sept 8) — Document the InsightLens → NexusFlow events_api dependency in the service catalog so downstream impact is visible during incidents.

AI-5 (Owner: Yuki Tanaka, Due: ongoing) — Reinforce blameless culture in the follow-up review. This post-mortem focuses on systems and process gaps, not individuals. Reviewers and approvers acted in good faith on the information available; our job is to make the right thing the easy thing.
