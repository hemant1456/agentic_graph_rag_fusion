Vertexia Inc. 2024 Strategic Plan
Authors: Arjun Mehta, CEO; Robert Okafor, CFO; Nadia Kim, CPO; Sarah Chen, CTO; Lisa Torres, CRO
Date: December 15, 2023

Overview
Following our successful $45M Series C raise in October 2023, Vertexia Inc. is entering a phase of aggressive scaling. With our headcount reaching ~500 employees and our ARR hitting $16.5M at the close of Q3 2023, we have established a robust foundation. Our product suite—NexusFlow (FLOW), InsightLens (LENS), and PulseConnect (PULSE)—is now market-tested. As we look toward 2024, our strategy is defined by four core pillars designed to transition Vertexia from a high-growth startup to an enterprise-grade market leader.

Priority 1: Enterprise Segment Expansion
We must leverage the success of our Phoenix Corp partnership ($2.4M ARR) to capture additional high-value enterprise accounts. While our standard NexusFlow architecture targets 99.9% uptime, our enterprise tier must consistently meet or exceed the 99.99% SLA required by clients like Phoenix Corp.
- Goal: Secure 20 new enterprise logos in 2024.
- OKR: Increase average contract value (ACV) by 35% through bundled product offerings (FLOW + LENS + PULSE).
- OKR: Reduce enterprise onboarding cycle time from 6 months to 3 months.

Priority 2: International Expansion (UK and Germany)
To diversify our revenue beyond the US market, we are initiating a formal expansion into Europe. We will establish localized sales and support hubs in London and Berlin by Q2 2024.
- Goal: Achieve $2M in international ARR by year-end 2024.
- OKR: Establish legal entities in the UK and Germany by May 2024; ensure full GDPR compliance across all data pipelines.
- OKR: Localize InsightLens UI and reporting features for German-speaking markets.

Priority 3: AI-Powered Product Evolution
We are moving beyond standard ETL and analytics to provide predictive business intelligence. Utilizing the data currently stored in our 90-day hot storage and 1-year cold S3 Glacier tiers, we will integrate machine learning models across our stack.
- Goal: Deploy AI-driven predictive forecasting modules in InsightLens by Q3 2024.
- OKR: Launch a beta version of "Pulse-AI" for automated CRM engagement scoring.
- OKR: Leverage GCP-based ML workloads to reduce data processing latency by 20% for NexusFlow.

Priority 4: Platform Unification and Technical Debt
Following the acquisition of DataCraft, we have successfully migrated from Apache Kafka to Apache Pulsar. However, full integration remains a priority to ensure technical parity across all teams. We must ensure the stability of the platform, learning from the lessons of the August 14, 2023, NexusFlow v2.1 outage—where the misconfigured rate limiter impacted both NexusFlow and the downstream InsightLens events_api.
- Goal: Finalize the DataCraft integration under the leadership of Felix Wagner and the Platform Engineering team.
- OKR: Achieve 99.99% system-wide reliability for all tier-one customers.
- OKR: Complete the migration of all legacy DataCraft modules to the unified Vertexia infrastructure by end of Q2 2024.

Financial Outlook
Our Q3 2023 performance ($4.12M GAAP revenue vs. $4.2M bookings) highlights our continued growth trajectory. While we recognize the delta between GAAP and bookings, our focus for 2024 is to align these metrics through stricter revenue recognition policies managed by Robert Okafor’s finance team. With the engineering organization now split into dedicated Platform and Product teams reporting to Sarah Chen, we are better positioned to balance rapid feature development with the stability required for our growing enterprise client base.

Conclusion
The path forward requires discipline. We have moved past the era of "Project Phoenix" (our Python 2→3 migration) and have matured into a company that powers the data infrastructure of some of the world's most demanding organizations. By focusing on enterprise acquisition, international presence, AI-driven value, and platform unification, we will solidify Vertexia’s position as an indispensable partner in the data ecosystem.

Arjun Mehta
CEO, Vertexia Inc.
