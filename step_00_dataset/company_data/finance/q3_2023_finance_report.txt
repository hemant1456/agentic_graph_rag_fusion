SUBJECT: Q3 2023 Financial Performance Report

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Vertexia Inc. concluded Q3 2023 with a solid financial performance, characterized by sustained growth in our core product lines following the organizational restructuring initiated by CTO Sarah Chen in April. Total GAAP recognized revenue for the quarter reached $4.12M, reflecting the increasing maturity of our enterprise deployments. While sales figures often highlight our $4.2M bookings performance, this report focuses exclusively on GAAP recognized revenue to ensure fiscal transparency and regulatory alignment.

2. REVENUE BREAKDOWN (GAAP)
Total GAAP revenue for Q3 2023 was $4.12M. The breakdown by product line is as follows:
- NexusFlow: $1.6M
- InsightLens: $1.5M
- PulseConnect: $1.02M

NexusFlow experienced the highest growth rate this quarter, driven by expanded usage within our existing enterprise base, including the Phoenix Corp account. Despite the 4-hour 23-minute service outage on August 14, 2023, which impacted both NexusFlow and downstream InsightLens analytics, we successfully maintained retention targets through proactive credit issuance managed by Maya Sharma’s Customer Success team.

3. MARGINS AND OPERATING EXPENSES
Our gross margin for the quarter stands at 72%. We are currently observing slight margin compression due to the increased cloud infrastructure costs associated with our migration from Apache Kafka to Apache Pulsar, a project spearheaded by the DataCraft Integration Team under Felix Wagner.

Operating expenses for Q3 were categorized as follows:
- R&D (Engineering & Product): $2.1M. This reflects the split of our engineering organization into Platform Engineering (led by Marcus Webb) and Product Engineering (led by Tomás García).
- Sales & Marketing: $1.1M. Costs are aligned with the ramp-up of the enterprise sales team reporting to Lisa Torres.
- G&A: $0.6M. Includes legal expenses overseen by Carmen Reyes and general corporate overhead.

4. CASH POSITION AND HEADCOUNT
Following the announcement of our Series C funding on October 5, 2023, which secured $45M in capital led by Sequoia, our cash position is strong. This capital injection is critical to supporting our global footprint across San Francisco, Austin, and Bangalore.

Headcount remains stable at approximately 500 employees as of the end of 2023. This includes the integration of the 12 employees who joined during the DataCraft acquisition in early 2022. Personnel costs remain our primary expense driver, with Zara Ahmed, VP of People & Culture, managing compensation benchmarking as we continue to compete for high-level engineering talent.

5. RISK AND COMPLIANCE
The outage on August 14, 2023, highlighted a gap between our internal NexusFlow architecture target of 99.9% uptime and the 99.99% SLA requirements mandated by our contract with Phoenix Corp. We are currently allocating additional budget to the Data Platform Team, specifically to support Kenji Ito and the on-call rotations, to ensure infrastructure reliability meets our contractual obligations.

6. OUTLOOK
With an ARR of $16.5M at the end of Q3 2023, Vertexia Inc. is well-positioned for the final quarter of the year. We will continue to prioritize GAAP-compliant growth and operational efficiency as we scale our infrastructure to meet the demands of our enterprise customer base.

Respectfully submitted,

Robert Okafor
CFO, Vertexia Inc.
