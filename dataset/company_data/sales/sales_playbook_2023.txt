2. IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP)
================================================================

Fit criteria:
- B2B, 200–5,000 employees
- Mid-market to lower enterprise (typical ACV: $80K–$400K)
- Data-driven culture: a named Head of Data, Analytics, or RevOps
- Multi-source data environment (Salesforce, Stripe, product DBs, marketing stack)
- Cloud-first (AWS or GCP); hybrid acceptable, on-prem-only is a pass
- Geos: North America (primary), UK/EU (secondary), India (Bangalore-led)

Disqualifiers:
- <100 employees (route to PLG self-serve waitlist)
- Regulated workloads requiring on-prem-only (HIPAA-heavy without cloud BAA path)
- No identified data owner

3. DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
================================================================

Lead with business pain, not product features.

Business context:
- "Walk me through how your team reports on revenue today — where does the data live before it lands in a dashboard?"
- "What decisions are blocked or delayed because the data isn't ready?"
- "How many analyst-hours per week go to data prep vs. analysis?"

Technical:
- "What's your current ingestion stack — Fivetran, Airbyte, in-house?"
- "Where does your BI layer sit — Tableau, Looker, Power BI, Mode?"
- "What's your warehouse — Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift?"
- "Any CDC or near-real-time requirements?"

Org & buying:
- "Who owns the budget for data tooling — Engineering, Data, or RevOps?"
- "When you last bought a tool in this category, what did procurement look like?"

4. PRODUCT POSITIONING
================================================================

NexusFlow vs. Fivetran
- We win on: hot-path performance (Go-based pipeline), Apache Pulsar-backed streaming for sub-minute freshness, and transparent per-pipeline pricing (Fivetran's MAR billing is a top buyer complaint).
- We tie on: connector breadth for top-50 SaaS sources.
- We lose on: long-tail niche connectors. Counter with our Connector SLA — net-new connector in 30 days for committed customers.
- Proof point: Phoenix Corp consolidated 3 ingestion tools onto NexusFlow in Q3 2022.

InsightLens vs. Tableau / Looker
- vs. Tableau: We're embedded-analytics-first. Tableau is desktop-heritage; InsightLens was built post-2021 for product teams shipping analytics inside their own apps. Faster time-to-embed, modern semantic layer.
- vs. Looker: LookML lock-in is real. InsightLens uses an open semantic layer compatible with dbt. We're typically 30–50% lower TCO at the mid-market band.
- Don't position InsightLens as a Tableau replacement for a 2,000-seat finance org. That's not our fight in 2023.

PulseConnect
- Position as the engagement layer that sits on top of NexusFlow + InsightLens. Strongest when the customer already has clean pipelines and wants action, not another CRM swap. Don't lead head-to-head vs. Salesforce — lead with "augment, don't replace."

"We're already on Fivetran + Tableau."
→ Don't try to rip-and-replace both. Land on the weaker leg (usually ingestion cost or embedded analytics gap) and expand.

"Price is high."
→ Anchor on analyst hours saved and freshness SLA. Discounting >15% requires Lisa Torres approval.

6. DEAL STAGES
================================================================

1. Prospect — fits ICP, no confirmed pain yet
2. Qualify — MEDDIC partially mapped, champion identified
3. Demo — tailored demo delivered, technical questions logged
4. Eval — POC or trial scoped with success criteria in writing
5. Negotiate — Legal/Procurement engaged, redlines in flight
6. Close — countersigned MSA, Maya's team takes handoff

Stage progression requires the next-stage exit criteria in Salesforce. No "happy ears" advancement.
